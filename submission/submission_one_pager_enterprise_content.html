Enterprise Content Ops Agent

Draft → Compliance → Human Approval → Localization → Multi-Channel Packaging
Problem
Enterprise content workflows are slow: drafting, legal/brand compliance, localization, and multi-channel packaging often involve iterative rework. The result is delayed turnaround time and inconsistent output.
Solution
  • Multi-agent pipeline that creates channel-specific drafts.
  • Compliance guardrails with required disclaimers + forbidden term checks + tone heuristics.
  • Human-in-the-loop approval gate with a compliance report before publishing.
  • Localization that preserves workflow consistency (demo uses simulated localization).
  • Packaging + scheduler that generates publish-ready payloads and a publish calendar.
What’s novel
  • End-to-end workflow automation (not single-step generation).
  • Auditable trace: every decision + score is saved under `runs/<job_id>/workflow_run.json`.
  • Consumer-like intelligence loop (simulated engagement scoring to choose hooks).
Impact Model
Back-of-envelope baseline per asset: ~6.75 hours manual lifecycle.
The demo estimates ~60% cycle-time reduction using guardrail + structured automation assumptions (math + assumptions stated in docs).
